Czech Dumplings Recipe

Description

This Czech dumplings recipe has been passed down for many generations in my family. My grandmother left me this recipe, and I consider it to be of great sentimental value. She said that it was her grandma Mamie's, which means that this recipe is from my great, great, grandmother.

I do not know the proper name for this dish, in fact, I'm not even entirely sure that this dish is technically of Czech origin. In my research, I discovered a Nordic meal called potato klubb, which appears to be nearly identical to these dumplings. However, one year, my family did a DNA test for fun, and it proved that there were no Norwegian ties on my grandma's side. Instead, she was mostly of Germanic European descent, which is where the Czech side comes from.

There are many variations of European dumpling dishes. For example, Kn√∂del are boiled dumplings that have ties to German, Czech, Austrian, and Slovak cuisine. They are definitely slightly different than potato klubb in that they often contain meat, plums, apricots, bread, egg yolk, etc, but they are similar in the simplicity of the recipes and use of potatoes. I digress, the recipe card that my grandma gave me is titled "My Grandma's Czech Dumplings," so I will assume that these are authentic Czech dumplings, and will refer to them as such.

When I was a kid, my Mom would make this dish for holidays and special occasions. She would serve the dumplings simply with butter, and I always loved them. Later in life, my wife and I were visiting my parents, and we decided to have the dumpling dish for dinner one night. My wife, who is part Taiwanese, was excited to try the dumplings. She had thoughts of flavorful Xiao Long Bao, Xiao Mai, or maybe at least something like gyoza. She was expecting a soft, doughy outside with some delicious pork, chicken, or maybe even crab or shrimp on the inside. I don't think she was prepared for the Czech potato dumplings that my ancestors weathered harsh winters with. It was a surprise for her, but a delicous one! We laughed and everyone enjoyed their dinner. The truth is, my wife is absolutely the foody of the family. She is an incredible cook. Aside from making a sandwich or whipping up some burgers for dinner, I am generally useless in the kitchen. I guess, I did learn to make sushi recently, but that is a story for another article.
