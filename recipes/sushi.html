Sushi Recipe

Description

Sushi is probably my favorite type of food. It is healthy, fun to eat, and (most importantly) delicious. I didn't like sushi as a kid because I was freaked out by the idea of eating raw fish. In college, my wife told me I needed to branch out, so I tried a California roll for first time, and it slowly grew on me. Fast forward nearly 10 years later, and I chose Nobu in Newport Beach for my 30th birthday celebration dinner (would highly recommend by the way).

If you've read any of my other articles, you'll probably already know that I am no Michelin Star chef. My wife once told me I should try out for the popular reality TV cooking show "Worst Cooks in America." All jokes aside, I've never worried about my lack of cooking skills because I prefer to focus on other skills instead. However, when I became a dad, I suddenly felt a great fear of being the father who couldn't cook a nice meal for his family. Sure, I'd love to have my wife's cooking every day for the rest of my life, and I would ie a happy man, but I didn't want to be a man who is completely incapable of preparing a nice meal for my family every so often. So I decided to learn to make sushi, because it seemed like a challenge, and I was curious to see how the sushi chefs actually prepare those tuna rolls for us behind the scenes.

After much trial and error, watching a bunch of cooking shows, and making sushi several times, I have compiled the following recipe for making delicious sushi rolls.
